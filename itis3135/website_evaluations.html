CRAP Principle Evaluations

Dave's Hot Chicken (Good Design)

  • Contrast: Clear contrast between background, text, and buttons so important info stands out and is easy to read.
  • Repetition: Consistent colors, typography, and button styles across the site, which makes it feel unified and professional.
  • Alignment: Content follows a clean layout/grid, so sections line up nicely and feel organized.
  • Proximity: Related items (menu info, locations/actions) are grouped together, making the site easy to scan quickly.

Webpages That Suck: Worst Websites of 2008 (Bad Design)

  • Contrast: The page is visually busy and distracting, so it’s harder to tell what’s most important at first glance.
  • Repetition: The overall page feels cluttered and inconsistent because there are many competing elements instead of a repeated, predictable style.
  • Alignment: Layout feels scattered; items don't follow a clean alignment pattern, which makes it look messy.
  • Proximity: Too much content is packed together, so unrelated items can feel mixed and harder to read/scan.
